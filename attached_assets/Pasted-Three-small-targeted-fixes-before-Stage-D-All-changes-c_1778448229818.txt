FIX 1 — Deterministic tiebreaker in imbalance penalty sort

In the rebalanced sort, replace the current single-key sort:

    rebalanced = sorted(
        survey_type_result.all_eligible_questions,
        key=lambda opt: opt.relevance_score - _imbalance_penalty(opt, st.session_state.decoded_df),
        reverse=True,
    )

With a three-key tiebreaker:

    rebalanced = sorted(
        survey_type_result.all_eligible_questions,
        key=lambda opt: (
            -(opt.relevance_score - _imbalance_penalty(opt, st.session_state.decoded_df)),
            _imbalance_penalty(opt, st.session_state.decoded_df),
            opt.question_id,
        ),
    )

Tiebreaker logic (descending effective score → ascending penalty → 
ascending question_id):
- First key: highest effective score wins (negated for ascending sort)
- Second key: on a tie, lower penalty wins (less imbalanced)
- Third key: on a further tie, alphabetical question_id for determinism

This ensures Q42 (penalty 0.40) loses to vos (penalty 0.20) on a tie, 
and vos loses to any zero-penalty question on a further tie.

FIX 3 — Penalise short question_text in relevance scoring

at the very top of the function before any keyword matching:
